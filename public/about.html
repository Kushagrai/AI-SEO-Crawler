AI SEO Crawler

A free, open audit tool that measures how visible your website is to AI-powered search engines — and tells you exactly how to improve it.

What This Tool Does

AI SEO Crawler fetches your website, follows internal links to the depth you specify, and evaluates four areas that determine whether AI systems can find, read, and cite your content accurately:

  1. Robots.txt access — checks whether major AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, and four others) are explicitly allowed or blocked.
  2. Sitemap coverage — validates that a well-formed sitemap.xml exists and contains your pages.
  3. Semantic HTML quality — verifies H1 headings, meta descriptions, canonical links, sufficient word count, and descriptive image alt text on every crawled page.
  4. Schema.org structured data — detects JSON-LD markup and identifies high-value schema types that enable AI systems to extract facts directly from your pages.

The output is a 0–100 score with a letter grade, a ranked list of issues across all pages, and prioritised recommendations.

Why AI SEO Is Different from Traditional SEO

Traditional search engines rank pages by matching keywords and measuring authority signals such as backlinks. AI search engines — including ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude — work differently. They read and summarise content, then cite the sources they consider most reliable and machine-readable.

For your site to appear in AI-generated answers, three things must be true:

  • AI bots must be permitted to crawl your site via robots.txt.
  • Your content must be structured clearly enough for a language model to extract facts, entities, and relationships without ambiguity.
  • Your pages must carry Schema.org JSON-LD that tells AI systems what type of content each page contains and who is responsible for it.

This tool audits all three areas in a single pass and scores them so you can prioritise fixes by impact.

How the Score Is Calculated

ROBOTS.TXT
20 pts
Full marks when AI bots are explicitly allowed and the robots.txt file is valid and accessible. Points are deducted for missing files, broad blocks, or undefined access for key AI crawlers.
SITEMAP
20 pts
Full marks when a valid sitemap.xml exists at the standard path, is well-formed XML, and contains enough URLs to represent the site's content.
SEMANTICS
35 pts
Averaged across all crawled pages. Checks for: one H1 per page, meta description (50–160 chars), canonical link, 300+ word body content, and alt text on all images.
SCHEMA.ORG
25 pts
Rewards presence and variety of JSON-LD structured data. High-value types (Article, FAQPage, Product, Organization, HowTo) score additional bonus points.

Frequently Asked Questions

What crawl depth should I use?

Depth 2 is the recommended default. It crawls the seed page and all pages directly linked from it, giving a representative sample of the site without excessive crawl time. Use Depth 3 for a thorough audit of larger sites. Use Depth 1 to quickly test a single page.

Does this tool modify my website?

No. The crawler is read-only. It sends standard HTTP GET requests exactly as a browser or search engine bot would. It does not submit forms, create accounts, or write any data to your site.

Why is Schema.org structured data important for AI?

Schema.org JSON-LD provides machine-readable facts embedded directly in page HTML. AI systems use these facts to understand content type, authorship, dates, prices, ratings, and relationships between entities — allowing them to answer user questions accurately and cite sources with confidence. Pages without structured data force AI models to infer meaning from prose alone, which is less reliable and less likely to result in a citation.

What if my robots.txt blocks AI bots?

Blocking AI bots via robots.txt prevents them from crawling your content. This means your pages will not appear in AI-generated answers, summaries, or citations from those systems. The crawler identifies which bots are blocked and flags this in the Robots.txt score and recommendations. Whether to allow or block specific bots is a business decision — this tool reports the current state so you can make an informed choice.

How accurate is the score?

The score measures the technical signals that AI search engines are known to use when deciding whether to crawl, index, and cite content. It does not measure content quality, domain authority, or whether AI systems have already indexed your site. A high score indicates strong technical foundations; it does not guarantee citation. Conversely, a low score identifies concrete technical barriers that are within your control to fix.

Structured Data on This Page
